Asset-based wealth estimation

Four questions to estimate household wealth in sub-Saharan Africa.

The DHS Wealth Index asks about 30+ household assets to place families on a wealth gradient. In acute clinical care, trauma registries, or rapid surveys, that's not feasible. EconomicClusters identifies the four asset questions that, for each country, separate households into the most distinct wealth groups. The wealth groups are validated against metrics commonly used to estimate wealth, including child mortality, women's education, and primary healthcare source.

How it works

From about 30 asset variables to 4, without losing the wealth signal.

For each DHS country, we filter the household survey down to 20–40 candidate asset variables (electronics, structural features, livestock, financial access), then exhaustively cluster every possible 4-variable subset using weighted Partitioning Around Medoids with a Hamming distance metric.

The subset producing the most well-separated clusters, measured by average silhouette width (ASW), becomes the country's recommended 4-question survey. We then rank-order the resulting household groups by their average asset count to construct five wealth ranks, and validate those ranks against three independent wealth indicators.

Click into any country below to see its top-performing variable sets, the wealth groups they produce, and how strongly those groups predict known wealth outcomes.

Cluster method

Weighted PAM (k=5) over Hamming dissimilarity, scored by average silhouette width.

Variable filtering

Drop variables with >10% missingness, >90% concentration in one category, or composite redundancy.

Validation

Ordered logistic regression of cluster wealth rank against child mortality, women's education, and primary healthcare source.

Reproducibility

Implementation in R; full pipeline and per-country outputs open-sourced on GitHub.

Data source

All survey data and questionnaires are drawn from IPUMS DHS, the integrated archive of Demographic and Health Surveys. Each country page links to the original household questionnaire used.
